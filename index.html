Khushdeep Singh

I am a MSc candidate with EIT Digital Master School. My Major is Autonomous Systems and minor is Innovation and Entrepreneurship. I am currently pursuing my masters' thesis at Bethge Lab on 'Improving Robustness in Reinforcement Learning using self-supervised learning', working with PhD candidate Steffen Schneider.

Being part of dual degree with integrated mobility program, my entry university was KTH Royal Institute of Technology and exit university is TU Berlin

Recently, I worked at FZI, a research organization as a research assistant in Berlin. Previously, I worked with a startup Centre for Robolution as a research engineer. I also worked with Prof. Guido Bugmann at University of Plymouth, United Kingdom, as a research intern.

I earned my bachelors' degree from Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology. Also, I was core committee member of IvLabs, a robotics club at VNIT.

Research

My research interest lies in the general area of Reinforcement Learning (RL), more specifically in meta-reinforcement learning (meta-RL) and continual learning.

An Open-Source Tele-Operated MobileManipulator: CHAP V1


Khushdeep Singh, Guido Bugmann, Dominic Cassidy, Paul Doyle, Arunaganesan Swaminathan
Towards Autonomous Robotic Systems conference, 2017
project page / paper / video

Teleoperated mobile manipulators are of use for disabled people and for the wider public interested in acting at distance. The high price of existing devices is a barrier to their diffusion. The paper reports on the first design produced in the Cheap Arm Project (CHAP).It costs less than Â£2000, uses easily available parts and can be assem-bled by anybody with basic technical skills. The manipulator can reach objects from floor-level up to shelves at a height of 170cm using a newlow-cost arm design. Teleoperation is be done using a tablet, smartphoneor browser.

Design Analysis and Development of Low Cost Under actuated Robotic Hand
Khushdeep Singh, Parag Khanna, K.M. Bhurchandi, Shital Chiddarwar
arXiv, 2020
paper / video

The project presents the control and driving mechanism of Robotic hand, performing various types of grasps while retaining the resemblance of a human hand. The hand is light weight, cost-effective and provide ease of attachment and control for people, having minimum wear and tear.
